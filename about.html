About

I care about what happens beneath the abstraction.

I’m Sooryashankar Joy, a Computer Science student at Purdue University focused on embedded systems, systems programming, and hardware–software interfaces. I’m drawn to the layers where timing, signals, and memory behavior shape what software can actually do.

I enjoy building systems where you can validate correctness with a measurement: a stable waveform, a consistent interrupt rate, a controller that converges, or a kernel that improves because you reduced cache misses.

Aiming for: embedded/systems roles where careful engineering, debugging, and fundamentals matter.
