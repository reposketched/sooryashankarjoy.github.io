Understanding Cache Miss Behavior

When performance surprises you, it’s often memory. A small change in access pattern can move a workload from “mostly L1 hits” to “waiting on DRAM”. I use perf counters to stay honest about where time goes. 

$ perf stat -e cycles,instructions,cache-misses ./kernel_bench

Key idea: optimize what you can measure; don’t infer cache behavior from intuition alone.

Designing Stable Op-Amp Circuits

Stability problems don’t look like “a little noise” — they look like a circuit that becomes its own signal generator. Before changing values randomly, I check the topology, loading, and whether the measurement setup is introducing oscillation.

Checklist: grounding, probe technique, bandwidth limits, and whether the op-amp is being asked to drive a capacitive load.

Debugging Microcontroller Timing Bugs

Timing bugs are rarely “mystical”. They’re usually one of: an incorrect clock assumption, an interrupt interaction, a race on a shared register, or a measurement artifact. I like to add one tiny piece of instrumentation at a time. 

GPIO high → ISR entry
GPIO low  → ISR exit
scope shows: latency + jitter
