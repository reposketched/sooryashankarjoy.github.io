Teaching

ECE 2k7 Teaching Assistant

In ECE 2k7 labs, students build and measure real circuits: breadboards, basic components, and instrumentation like oscilloscopes and multimeters. My goal is to help students connect what the circuit should do to what it actually does on the bench—and then debug the gap.

Purdue University Instrumentation Oscilloscope Debugging

Responsibilities

  • Guide students through lab procedures and setup without taking the work away from them.
  • Help debug wiring, grounding, and measurement issues (scope probes, reference points, noise).
  • Check circuit behavior against expectations and help students form a test plan.
  • Reinforce safe, careful bench habits and clean documentation.

What I help students learn

  • How to turn a schematic into a working breadboarded circuit.
  • How to measure signals correctly (what the instrument is actually telling you).
  • How to debug systematically: isolate, hypothesize, test, iterate.
  • How to reason about tolerances, loading, and non-ideal behavior.

My teaching philosophy

I try to make debugging feel less like guesswork and more like engineering. That means building a short list of plausible failure modes, running small experiments, and writing down what changed. Clear thinking beats panic every time.

Focus: fundamentals, careful measurement, and habits that transfer to bigger systems.

Tools in the lab

  • Oscilloscope (probing technique, triggering, bandwidth, noise)
  • Digital multimeter (continuity, voltage, current)
  • Function generator (stimulus and verification)
  • Breadboards, jumpers, and basic components (resistors, capacitors, diodes, op-amps)
